About me

I’m a computational biologist and Postdoctoral Fellow in Dr. Itai Yanai’s lab at NYU School of Medicine. My main research interest lies in developing and applying computational and statistical tools to help improve our understanding of gene regulatory processes. My current research focuses on the development of computational methods for the efficient analysis of single-cell RNA-Seq data. As part of this research, I have recently developed k-nearest neighbor smoothing (Wagner et al., 2018), a new method for reducing the high levels of technical noise characteristic of high-throughput scRNA-Seq data.

In 2017, I received my PhD in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics from Duke University. As part of my dissertation work, I developed GO-PCA (Wagner, 2015), a new method for systematic exploratory analysis of RNA-Seq data. In the past, I have also been involved in a project studying the development of the nematode C. elegans using microarray expression analysis of precisely staged embryos (Levin et al., 2012), and in the development of CEL-Seq (Hashimshony et al., 2012), an experimental method for linear amplification and high-throughput sequencing of mRNA from single cells.

What I enjoy most about doing research is learning new things all the time, thinking critically, being creative, solving technical challenges, and working collaboratively.
